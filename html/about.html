The Olympic Story

The **Ancient Olympic Games** were held in Olympia, Greece, from the 8th century BC to the 4th century AD. They were a religious and athletic festival that brought together city-states, observing a sacred truce for the duration of the games. This early tradition set the foundation: a temporary halt to conflicts in the name of athletic pursuit and human excellence.

The **Modern Olympic Movement** was revived by **Baron Pierre de Coubertin** in the late 19th century. His vision was to promote physical education and international peace through a grand sporting event. The first modern Games took place in Athens in **1896**, marking the rebirth of a global legacy.

Since then, the Games have grown into the world's foremost sporting event, expanding to include the **Winter Games** and the **Paralympic Games**. It stands as a beacon for inspiring billions, celebrating the extraordinary achievements of athletes, and promoting the values of respect, friendship, and excellence across borders. We continue to evolve, integrating new sports and embracing modern sustainability efforts to ensure the legacy lasts for generations.

Foundational Pillars

Global Solidarity

We believe in the unifying power of sport, transcending political, cultural, and geographic boundaries to foster a sense of global community.

Athlete Wellbeing

Our commitment is to protect and support athletes on and off the field, promoting clean sport, mental health awareness, and ethical conduct.

Environmental Action

We strive to ensure the Games are planned and delivered in an environmentally responsible manner, contributing to climate change and sustainability goals.

Social Responsibility

Using sport as a tool for social development, we promote gender equality, inclusion, and access to physical activity for all communities globally.

Mission & Core Values

Our Core Mission Statement:

To place **sport at the service of humanity** and to promote a peaceful society concerned with the preservation of human dignity. We aim to inspire and educate youth through sport practiced without discrimination, in a spirit of friendship, solidarity, and fair play.

Excellence

This calls upon the athletic spirit to give the best of oneself, both on the field of play and in professional life. It is about striving to reach one’s potential and celebrating the hard work of the journey.

Friendship

Encouraging mutual understanding and solidarity. It is the bond that links people together across the world, promoting a sense of unity and peace regardless of background or culture.

Respect

Upholding fair play, and respecting every individual—opponents, officials, and spectators. It also means valuing the cultural heritage and environment in which the Games take place.
